RETAINED · NCU-GUIDED2 Jul (late)

Counters overturned the latency-wall theory: the row builder is strided-access-bound

Your Nsight Compute report settled it: memory 66% vs compute 25% speed-of-light, and only 5.1 of 32 bytes utilized per global-load sector (5.8/32 for stores) — every [row, dof] access puts adjacent row-threads 144 bytes apart. 61% of stalls wait on those accesses; occupancy is secondarily register-limited (62.5%).

  • Retained (commit 3acd36f9): block-cooperative coalesced Jacobian zero-fill plus recomputing the source-path Jacobian dot for the effective mass instead of reloading the padded row per DOF. Bit-identical; all 40 reduced-articulation CUDA graph tests pass; builder 410.0 → 399.3 µs; physics-only runs at 1.22–1.24M env steps/s.
  • Measured but not retained: always routing responses through the tile transpose cuts the builder to 312.5 µs (−24%) and lifts physics-only throughput, but consistently costs 2.7% of steady leapfrog training — the extra per-substep transpose contends with the overlapped learner stream.
  • Strategic insight (corrected by a stream-balance trace): steady leapfrog training is rollout-bound — GPU union-busy is 96%, the rollout stream (~0.45 s/iter) dominates the update stream (~0.14 s/iter). Solver/collision wins are on the end-to-end critical path, but a few-percent physics gain is only ~1–2% end-to-end and sits inside bench noise; small candidates must be judged by rollout-stream busy time from nsys, not raw samples/s. The row builder also averages ~205 µs in real training vs ~400 µs in the standing-pose profile (fewer contacts mid-episode).
PHYSICS · PROFILE REFRESH2 Jul (late)

Contact-row builder confirmed as the top solver kernel; three fix hypotheses falsified

A fresh physics-only Nsight Systems trace after the state-continuation commit ranks the reduced-pipeline kernels: contact-row build 18.7% (410 µs/launch), ABA advance 12.8%, contact tile solve 12.9%, factorization 11.1%, publish 7.0%, contact gather 5.3%. The contact pipeline as a whole is ~38% of physics GPU time. Physics-only throughput is 1.189M env steps/s under the profiler, matching the headline.

  • A bit-identical scalar fast path for 1-DOF joints (skipping the unrolled 6×6 inertia loops) was neutral — the builder is not instruction-bound.
  • Replacing the global ancestor-response scratch with a per-thread depth-indexed stack (bit-identical, removes ~280 MB/launch of scratch traffic) regressed 25% — that traffic was already L2-absorbed; the local stack frame only added local-memory pressure. Reverted.
  • ptxas shows 64 registers, zero spills; a block-dim sweep (32–192) is flat — occupancy is not register- or shape-limited. Row-count scaling is strongly sub-linear (12 basis rows: 349 µs vs 24 rows: 462 µs), so the cost is per-thread serial tree-traversal latency.
  • The 2026-06 unit-wrench basis compression prototype was re-measured against today's 36-wide packed layout: only 1.12× on build+solve (down from the recorded ~19%), with FP-tolerance rather than bit-exact impulses. Not worth productionizing; the stale-width benchmark was fixed and the result recorded in PERF_NOTES.
  • The factorization kernel (168 registers, ~12 warps/SM) was given the advance kernel's sub-warp articulation packing — 4 G1 trees per warp, one occupancy wave instead of 3.6: +9% slower. Four independent experiments now agree the reduced pipeline sits at a dependent memory-latency wall that added concurrency does not cross.

Mission and non-negotiables

  • Main quest: maximize end-to-end PhoenX G1 RL training throughput while keeping repeatable successful training. Faster unsuccessful training does not count.
  • Optimize the reduced-coordinate path where training currently succeeds; improving maximal-coordinate training remains valuable, but do not abandon the proven walker.
  • Keep physics clean and physically grounded: conserve momentum, preserve reliable convergence, retain the hard-Hertz contact model where possible, and use no scene-specific hacks.
  • End-to-end CUDA graph capture is mandatory. Classic PhoenX must remain deterministic.
  • Never overfit to G1. Screen Anymal, H1/G1-class robots, contact-rich scenes, closed loops, mixed ordinary joints/constraints, multiple articulations per world, and both single- and multi-world execution.
