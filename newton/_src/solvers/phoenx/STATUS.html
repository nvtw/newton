PHYSICS WIN · DOF-TYPE FACTOR SPLIT16 Jul

Occupancy unlock: split the factor by joint-DOF-type — +2.82% physics, the biggest lever since fp16x2

The depth-major ABA rejection established the 50%-of-GPU reduced-dynamics phase is register-bound, not launch-bound (factor kernel 144 regs → ~22% occupancy). Followed that pointer to a clean, generic win.

  • Localized the register wall: the single-DOF revolute path needs only 64 regs (50% occ), but the one free-base lane’s 6×6 SPD invert adds +80, dragging the whole warp to 144/22%. Every in-place reduction rejected with compile-time data (Schur 3×3-block invert 149, in-place Cholesky 147, recompute 148 — all > 144): the peak is the multi-DOF branch’s aggregate output footprint, not the invert internals. Solve-not-invert also rejected (the inverse is reused as a matrix across advance/impulse-response/contact-solve). Only physically removing the branch helps.
  • The split: single-DOF joints reduce in a lean 64-reg / 50%-occ depth-walk kernel; the 6×6 root invert defers to a companion launch. Generic DOF-count dispatch (mirrors the existing revolute specialization), active only when every multi-DOF joint is an articulation root; mid-chain multi-DOF safely falls back. No scene identity baked in.
  • Measured (solo GPU, reverse A/B, drop trial0, 8 trials): factor kernel 112.6→69.3 µs = 1.62× (+38.4%) @8192, 1.30× @4096. Full-step graph_leapfrog: +2.82% @8192, +1.28% @4096 (every ON sample > every OFF sample). Occupancy 22→50% converted. Biggest physics lever measured this campaign (fp16x2 was +1.06%).
  • Critical fix: the companion roots kernel must launch block_dim=32 — the default 256 packs the few heavy 6×6 roots onto ~16 blocks and stalls (~2.5×), regressing 4096 by −36%. With 32 it spreads across SMs and the split is a strict win at both counts (no world-count gate).
  • Exact + tested: bit-identical to the unified kernel (max_abs_diff 0.0 across 40 floating-base articulations, graph-captured, deterministic); new CUDA-graph regression test pins split==unified; floating-base momentum/energy + Featherstone-parity battery passes.

Data-directed physics innovation: depth-major → register-bound diagnosis → localized to the free-base lane → in-place reformulations (incl. Schur) ruled out with compile-time data → DOF-split. Awaiting user go to commit. Session banked physics: fp16x2 +1.06% and now factor-split +2.82%.

BIG-SWING REJECT · AMP MOTION PRIOR16 Jul

AMP does not lift p / time-to-policy on G1 — the gate is tracking-dominated

The user-chosen big swing — Adversarial Motion Priors (Peng 2021), motion-prior RL to fix seed reliability — was built end-to-end (discriminator to 0.999 held-out accuracy, LSGAN + style reward + DeepMimic anneal, CUDA-graph-safe, deterministic) and evaluated decisively. Clean reject on the primary metric.

  • 6-seed iter-75 screen, within-run baseline mean 0.583. NO AMP arm beats it: w_style 0.5=0.525, 0.3=0.512, 0.2=0.529, 0.1=0.582, anneal(1.0→0)=0.542. As w→0 it converges to baseline; no weight exceeds it. 0/6 pass the 0.90 gate in every arm.
  • Mechanism (textbook style-vs-task tension): AMP slashes falls (seed 42: 8→0, 47: 19→0) and smooths gait (leg_qvel_rms ~0.08 vs baseline ~0.25–0.53) — the policy visibly walks more like the reference — but velocity-command tracking (the gate metric) drops (mean lin-vel error 0.399→0.459). The discriminator rewards looking-like-reference, which pulls off the commanded velocities. Anneal’s pure-style start destabilized seeds 29 (4→330 falls) and 71 (44→118) as easily as it helped seed 89.
  • Silver lining (documented): AMP is a real robustness / variance-reduction / fall-cutting prior (std 0.100→0.055–0.075) — it would help a robustness-gated task. G1’s gate is tracking-dominated, so it is not the lever here. Code + reproducible reference/scripts persisted (out of /tmp); nothing committed.

Three seed-reliability approaches now closed with 6-seed data: entropy floor, gSDE, and AMP. Walking must be discovered and tracked; a style prior guides toward reference-like motion but trades away the command-tracking the gate scores. The p lever remains open; physics-throughput is where this session banked (fp16x2, factor-split).

SEED-VARIANCE · EXPLORATION FIXES FAIL15 Jul

Standard RL exploration levers do not reduce G1 seed variance

G1 training p(success)≈1/3 (baseline 6-seed iter-75: mean 0.633, std 0.077, 2/6 pass 0.68 — replicated). PhoenX exploration is temporally-independent Gaussian with a collapsing global std and entropy coeff 1e-5, the textbook high-variance setup. Two established SOTA fixes were implemented and swept over 6 seeds:

  • Entropy floor + anneal (coeff 3e-3→1e-5, log_std_min −1.0): worse — mean 0.605, std 0.116 (WIDER). Forcing exploration magnitude stops seeds committing to a gait.
  • gSDE (Raffin 2021 correlated state-dependent noise, validated impl): collapses the spread (std 0.077→0.012) but caps the mean at ~0.48–0.49 (0/6 pass) at EVERY magnitude swept (log_std_init −1.5/−2.0/−2.5, including eff_std≈1). Not a calibration issue — correlated noise fundamentally under-explores the walking basin for G1; no magnitude is both tight AND high.
  • Conclusion: G1 seed variance is NOT fixable by changing exploration NOISE (structure or magnitude) — the problem is that walking must be DISCOVERED, and neither white nor correlated noise discovers it reliably. This motivates a motion PRIOR (AMP): reward walking-like motion to guide all seeds to the walking basin. Per the primary metric (time-to-policy, p dominates), AMP is the seed-reliability escalation; it is scoped feasible pure-Warp. Learner WIP reverted.

Both exploration interventions rejected with 6-seed data. The one untested learner lever is update-side stability (KL-bounded early-stop, trim replay-ratio/epochs). Solver side is at its GPU-friendly ceiling (APGD, block-GS, iteration/substep reduction all rejected).

INNOVATION · EXACT IMPLICIT MAXIMAL DRIVE15 Jul

Diagnosed the maximal drive-softness — feasible ~2-line fix unlocks the 1.8× solver

After the incremental reduced-pipeline levers were exhausted (bandwidth, row-count, iteration reduction — the recipe is near-minimal), the real lever is the coordinate system: maximal does the same physics ~1.8× faster per step but fails G1 training on soft/laggy drives. A controlled diagnostic pinned the exact cause and the fix.

  • Root cause (measured): the softness is not the drive row or joint coupling — single joints and pinned chains are mode-identical. It appears only on a FLOATING multi-link tree with explicit torque: distal rise time 480 ms (maximal) vs 80 ms (reduced) = 6× lag; maximal@2-iters collapses to 0.099 vs reduced 0.591, and does not converge even at 32 iterations. Reduced folds the implicit PD into the ABA factorization and solves it exactly per substep; the projector does an exact O(tree) solve but has NO drive term, leaving the drive to under-converged PGS.
  • The fix is the exact analog of reduced’s implicit PD, in the projector recursion: add the implicit-PD impedance to the driven-DOF diagonal of the joint block (d_matrix[i,i] += dt·kd + dt²·ke, mirroring reduced’s factor_diagonal += dt·damping + dt²·stiffness) so it propagates through the articulated inertia the parent sees, plus the drive force in the top-down RHS. One exact recursion → identical crispness to reduced, zero iteration dependence. ~2 lines in the projector kernel + drive-input plumbing.
  • Validated: the implicit drive collapses the floating-base tracking gap ~0.015→~0.002 rad (7× crisper, matches reduced) at 2 iterations. Committed opt-in (default OFF byte-identical).
  • Projected-maximal now TRAINS G1 (2 seeds). With crisp drives (constraint_drive, iter-75 screen): maxproj 0.580/0.560 (s42/s11) vs same-actuation reduced 0.631/0.641 — versus 0.255 with the old soft drives. Drive softness WAS the dominant blocker (0.255→~0.57), a genuine scientific result: the maximal path is now trainable at all.
  • Trainability confirmed at ~parity (3 seeds). maxproj+implicit-drive 0.580/0.560/0.593 (mean 0.578) vs same-actuation reduced 0.631/0.641/0.538 (mean 0.603) — ~96%, within seed noise (seed 29 maxproj beats reduced). Drive softness, not contacts, was the blocker.
  • But the trainable mode’s throughput ceiling is only 1.12×. Clean physics (8192 G1): reduced 2.1M, projected-maximal 2.4M = 1.12×; the factor-scale 2.36× belongs to PURE maximal (non-articulated independent bodies) which cannot train a robot. The tree-projection step that makes maximal trainable also eats most of its speed advantage. So the innovation is real and correct but caps at ~10% physics for G1 — not the factor-scale win hoped, and offset by a constraint_drive actuation tax (both arms ~0.60 vs 0.707 explicit_torque). Net G1 time-to-policy: ~neutral unless the actuation quality is recovered.
  • Retained: the crisp implicit-drive algorithm (committed, opt-in) — a genuine unblock of maximal trainability, with more upside on contact-rich robots where maximal’s per-step edge is larger than G1’s 1.12×. Session banked win: fp16x2 +1.06%.

DEFINITIVE REJECT · PATCH ROWS15 Jul

Row-count reduction is the wrong lever for the reduced contact solve

Build-time patch-reduced rows (P+2 coupled tangents per convex column instead of 3 per point) was pursued through three milestones after the earlier separate-phase attempt (which lost −7.4%). It is now definitively rejected, with a clean architectural root cause.

  • The row reduction is real and physically correct: G1 is 100% patch-eligible, 30–33% fewer generalized rows realized, box-on-plane stable, momentum/energy pass, deviation vs per-point < 5×10−3, default OFF byte-identical.
  • But it costs −35% isolated G1 physics (1.40M vs 2.17M steps/s) even in the pure-patch, fully-activated, single-purpose-kernel form (M3, which removed the M2 dual-dispatch occupancy bloat). NEW AUTHORITATIVE METRIC4 Jul

Cold-start wall-clock time to a qualified policy

The clock starts immediately before launching the training process with no policy, replay, optimizer, normalization, or curriculum state. It stops at the first held-out evaluation that satisfies the task gate. Environment/model creation, normal Warp cache loading, graph capture, rollout, learning, evaluation, checkpointing, and any population overhead are included.

  • Primary deployment clock: a normal ready installation with its persistent Warp kernel cache in the declared state. A cleared-JIT-cache first-install clock is reported separately so compiler noise cannot hide training improvements.
  • Success: task-specific thresholds are frozen before an optimization run, evaluated on held-out randomized commands/initial states, and must pass in consecutive evaluation windows. Failed runs are not dropped.
  • Expectation value: time-to-policy is a random variable. The primary estimate is expected wall-clock cost to success under a frozen restart policy: mean successful-run time plus mean charged failure time × (1−p)/p, where p is success probability. A predeclared early rejection is charged its observed time; crashes, timeouts, and ordinary misses are conservatively charged the full cutoff H. Also report uncertainty and raw seed outcomes.
  • Censoring: timed-out and failed runs are retained. They are not assigned the mean of successful runs; the restart-cost estimate explicitly penalizes unreliability. A configuration with no successes by H is unresolved/dominated, not silently excluded.
  • Sequential judgment: one run may reject only a correctness failure or overwhelming regression. Ordinary candidates get paired seeds and adaptive racing; uncertain results receive more runs. Finalists use enough independent seeds to estimate expected cost and uncertainty.
  • Seed protocol: vary seeds for policy initialization, environment/reset randomization, command sampling, and learner ordering. Compare candidates on the same paired development seed bank, then confirm on a separate unseen seed bank. Because PhoenX/PhoenX-RL is deterministic per seed, every trial is exactly reproducible and seed effects are not confused with runtime noise.
  • Evaluation budget: frequent cheap screening evaluations trigger the full held-out gate only when promising. Both screening and confirmation time count, and stopping rules are frozen to avoid cherry-picking.
