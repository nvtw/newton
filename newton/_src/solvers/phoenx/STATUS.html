EXACT ROW-BUILDER GAIN3 Jul - latest

Source-path scratch tracking raises G1 to 1.17M env steps/s

The generalized contact-row builder no longer clears projected ABA scratch for every articulation DOF and every contact row. Its reverse pass already writes every source-path value; the forward pass now follows the existing root-to-leaf topology path and reads only those freshly written entries.

  • Matched 8192-world, three-substep, 256-replay measurements improve from 1.146M to 1.166M and 1.170M environment steps/s, a repeatable 1.7-2.1% exact physics gain.
  • No equation, response, effective mass, contact model, iteration count, or timestep changes. Non-path joints still receive the exact ABA parent response with a zero local projected impulse.
  • The ABA row-reference test now uses a branched articulation whose contact path skips a multi-DOF sibling branch. Packed Jacobians, generalized responses, virtual work, and effective mass agree with the independent reference.
  • All 38 reduced-articulation CUDA graph tests pass, including dense and sparse contacts, arbitrary page counts, self-contact, closed loops, multiple articulations per world, deterministic dense contacts, spatial momentum, translation invariance, and Featherstone comparisons.
  • A 32-thread sparse row-builder launch was also tested and removed: it measured 1.137M steps/s, slower than the retained baseline.

Assessment: this is a general dataflow optimization for every block-owned reduced articulation contact. It is graph-stable, deterministic, requires no tuning, and preserves physical results.

LAYOUT + SCHEDULE AUDIT2 Jul - latest

Advance is dependency-bound; storage and scheduling shortcuts are rejected

The retained 8192-world, 5x2x1 reduced G1 graph measures 624.8k environment steps/s under the bounded profiler. Every candidate below preserved the exact ABA and hard-Hertz equations; slower candidates were removed completely.

  • A production-shaped adaptive twelve-wrench response basis reached only 598k steps/s, 4.3% below baseline. Its isolated matrix synthesis win did not repay classification, scratch buffers, extra launches, and solve-path branching.
  • Atomic compaction of active contact rows reached 585k steps/s. Nsight showed compaction itself cost only 10.6 microseconds, but random articulation ordering destroyed factor/topology locality and raised the row builder from about 465 to 614 microseconds.
  • A locality-preserving 32-thread row block was also rejected at 615-624k steps/s. The existing 96-thread block remains best; inactive lanes cost less than losing articulation-local execution and cache reuse.
  • The frozen 125.8M walker proves that two PGS iterations are required: 5x1 produced 69 battery falls, and a 6x1 temporal schedule still produced 8 falls in a short 3,000-sample battery. Solver work cannot simply be deleted.
  • SOR cannot replace the second sweep: one-iteration boosts of 1.2, 1.4, and 1.6 produced 25, 35, and 48 falls respectively in the same bounded battery, with severe torso angular motion. The temporary G1 SOR plumbing was removed.
  • Combining external and gyroscopic forces before PGS removes the second ABA advance and raises the physics graph to 665.1k steps/s (+6.4%), but it was rejected: long-horizon and hybrid floating-tree linear momentum drifted 5.6% and 3.9%, and loop momentum exceeded tolerance. The split dynamics and exact momentum correction remain load-bearing.
  • A fresh full-training Systems trace is physics dominated: reduced advance 13.6%, contact rows 11.8%, publication 10.9%, factorization 9.9%, and generalized contact solve 7.3%. Policy forward is about 1.4%, so learner-only tuning cannot close the current gap while graph-leapfrog overlap is active.
  • The focused advance NCU report shows the real limit: 75.9% of issue delay is long-scoreboard waiting on global/L1TEX dependencies, only 13.5% of scheduler cycles have an eligible warp, achieved occupancy is 22.4%, and only 12.3 lanes are active on average. DRAM is 38.8% utilized, with 75.3% L1 and 84.8% L2 hit rates, so raw bandwidth and read-only cache routing are not the bottleneck.
  • Explicit native shared memory was proven first with a block-isolation, alignment, and repeated CUDA-graph test. The exact three-buffer ABA staging then regressed 3.2%; a 12 KB velocity-only staging still regressed 1.1%. Shared-address/indirection cost and residency outweigh the avoided loads in the current packed layout, so all production code and the now-unused test were removed.
  • Four-lane articulation groups regressed 2.4% by shrinking the grid and serializing wide depths. Moving the sole generalized-RHS consumer beside its producer was neutral/slower. The retained eight-lane global-memory traversal remains best among these exact variants.
  • An exact fused initialization/factorization pass was rejected at 1.469M versus 1.511M physics steps/s (-2.8%): it removed a launch but serialized independent transform and motion-subspace work inside the dependency-heavy backward traversal.
  • A closed-form COM spatial-inertia transform replaced two generic 6x6 products with the exact rigid-body block formula. It reduced initialization by only about 1-2 microseconds and was end-to-end neutral, so the floating-point reorder was not retained.
  • Retained gain: bias-free relaxation now publishes velocities only, reusing the configuration and motion subspaces produced immediately beforehand. Nsight reduces the second publication from about 116-118 to 31 microseconds; the G1 graph rises from 624.8k to 648.3k steps/s (+3.8%).
  • The gain is not G1-specific. Exact 64-robot A/Bs improve Anymal by 1.6-2.3%, H1 by 2.3-4.0%, and G1 by 3.0-3.9% across single- and multi-world layouts. A direct CUDA-graph oracle finds bitwise-identical generalized coordinates, generalized velocities, body poses, and body velocities versus full publication.
  • prepare_refresh_stride was silently forced to one for reduced/hybrid articulations, and reduced contact blocks ignored the refresh decision. Both paths are now plumbed correctly for internal SolverPhoenX substeps, including block, deferred, and fallback contacts. A CUDA-graph regression verifies the exact prepare/cache cadence.
  • The current G1 environment performs collision plus a one-substep solver call externally, so automatic stride remains one and training physics is unchanged. Reusing rows across those changing collision sets would be physically unsafe.

Next: target fewer or shorter dependency chains rather than cache hints, shared-memory copies, or lane-width tuning. Candidate algorithms must preserve split gyroscopic dynamics and exact momentum, then pass Anymal/H1/G1, sparse/dense contacts, single/multi world, and multiple-articulations-per-world screening.

EXACT BASIS PROTOTYPE2 Jul - latest

Tile synthesis works; standalone adaptive overhead must be fused away

The response-basis idea is now measured against live G1 ABA factors and contact wrenches, not a projection. Two contacted feet require twelve unit-wrench responses to reconstruct all 24 normal/tangent rows.

  • A packed 24x12 by 12x96 tile multiply synthesizes Jacobian and response together in 31-34 microseconds for 8192 worlds. It is 2.4-2.8x faster than scalar packed synthesis and bitwise identical to that scalar matrix product.
  • On live ABA data, basis construction plus tile synthesis takes 396 microseconds versus 521 microseconds for direct row construction, a 1.32x isolated speedup. Jacobians are exact; maximum response difference is 3.22e-6 from the changed floating-point summation order.
  • A naive production layout is rejected: separate GPU classification and unpacking raise the adaptive total to 578 microseconds. No slower path has been enabled.
  • The clean route is to classify the bounded body basis inside the existing one-block contact gather and let generalized PGS consume the combined row-major Jacobian/response matrix directly. That removes both measured overhead kernels and stays graph-safe.
  • A six-right-hand-side-per-thread ABA experiment was also rejected immediately: register pressure made it over four times slower at only 64 worlds. Independent basis rows with 16-lane articulation groups remain the viable form.

Expected scope: this can remove roughly one quarter of row-construction time on pages where several contacts share one or two bodies. The direct exact builder remains necessary for sparse pages and high body diversity, so the dispatch must be automatic and scene-general.
